# Style Packs

Use style packs as defaults, not rigid templates. Prefer the user's requested
style when one is given.

## clean-docs

For README, documentation, articles, and product explainers.

- White or very light background.
- Sparse shapes, clear hierarchy, restrained color.
- Labels are short and readable.
- No stock-photo feeling.
- No decorative gradients or blobs.

## technical-minimal

For architecture, RPC, database, and system diagrams.

- Precise boxes, arrows, lanes, boundaries, and stores.
- Limited palette: black/gray plus one accent color.
- Use labels for entities and payloads.
- Prefer clarity over personality.
- Avoid decorative illustration.

## review-minimal

For code review findings and risk visuals.

- Neutral base with one warning accent.
- Show the risky transition or missing guard.
- Keep severity visual but not melodramatic.
- Good for bug path, regression path, and test gap map.

## editorial-sketch

For conceptual article illustrations.

- Hand-drawn or sketch-like, but not childish.
- Fresh metaphor, one core idea.
- Plenty of white space.
- Sparse labels.
- Do not use third-party recurring characters.

## simple-doodle

For friendly explainers, lightweight technical concepts, and short-video
knowledge content.

- White background, simple marker-like outlines.
- Objects are reduced to the fewest recognizable lines.
- One or two accent colors.
- Handwritten labels are allowed but short.
- Keep it charming and clear, not childish or messy.

## cartoon-explainer

For concepts that benefit from approachable characters, reactions, or small
story scenes.

- Rounded simplified objects and expressive props.
- Characters may appear, but do not use third-party IP.
- Use 1-3 panels or one clear scene.
- Keep technical labels readable.
- Avoid meme faces, childish cuteness, and over-rendered cartoons.

## whiteboard-comic

For engineering stories, bug explanations, before/after behavior, and teaching
moments.

- Whiteboard-style black line art.
- Small arrows, callouts, and motion marks.
- Works well for 2-4 panel sequences.
- Sparse color accents for problem/result.
- Avoid dense handwritten paragraphs.

## sticker-layer

For playful video-ready visuals that can be animated as separate objects.

- Flat sticker-like shapes with clean outlines.
- Clear foreground/midground/background layers.
- Big isolated objects that can pop in or slide.
- Labels feel like stickers or small tags.
- Avoid glossy 3D, busy collage, and tiny details.

## soft-clay-cartoon

For friendly concept metaphors and video inserts.

- Soft rounded 3D-like objects, clay or toy-like, but restrained.
- Simple lighting, clean background, no photorealism.
- Few large objects, strong silhouette.
- Very little text.
- Avoid childish toy scenes when the topic is technical.

## product-explainer

For product docs and feature explanations.

- Calm SaaS/docs look.
- One product capability per visual.
- Use realistic but simplified UI surfaces only when the UI is central.
- Avoid landing-page hero gloss unless explicitly requested.
